Design as an Iterative Process

Based on our research findings from the spring semester, during the summer, our team organized our design work using an iterative and agile approach. We fit each iteration into a sprint where we ideate, build, test, and validate within 14 days.

We adjusted Google’s sprint plan to fit our team’s needs, so that we could fit 4 design sprints into our 9-week summer semester. Using a sprint plan helped us to move fast and direct our action towards making things, instead of discussing or pondering over certain decisions indefinitely. In each iteration, we built prototypes based on the last round of input from users and tested with users again. In this process, we were able to continuously gain deeper insights into the true needs of our users and build a system that they would truly enjoy using.


This sprint plan allowed us to fit ideation, prototype building, user testing, and calibration into 2 weeks rapidly. Each sprint was built upon the previous ones, in that we inherited findings and testing results from previous sprints and used them to guide our design decisions.
