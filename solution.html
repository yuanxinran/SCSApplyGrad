What final solution did we come up with?

How do we imagine the future of graduate application systems?

Why did the client come to us?

The Carnegie Mellon University School of Computer Science has designed, developed and deployed “ApplyGrad,” a system for managing applications to 23 Executive Education courses, 3 REU programs as well as 47 masters and doctoral graduate programs in computational biology, computer science, human-computer interaction, language technologies, machine learning, software engineering and robotics.

As the fields of machine learning and artificial intelligence continue to grow, so does the popularity of Computer Science graduate programs. Carnegie Mellon’s School of Computer Science is no exception. But with this popularity comes an increasing number of applicants; in the 2019–2020 application cycle, over 10,000 applications were submitted to SCS alone. While this is an exciting opportunity for the department, how can the graduate application process be improved to help address the growing workload for admissions reviewers as well as envision the next generation of graduate applications systems?

Client's Goals

Near-term Tactical and Long-Term Strategic
  • Identify usability improvement opportunities for Apply Grad and design improved interactions, workflows and patterns
  • Envision a system that creates a strategic advantage for SCS graduate programs and delivers benefits to CMU employees, applicants and alumni
  • Exploring the use of advanced technology to showcase advanced capabilities such as intelligence, automation, configurability and adaptivity to drive consensus among the community of captive users
