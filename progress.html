Research Overview

In the first phase of our project, the team focused on understanding ApplyGrad and the admissions process by departments. We probed into the needs of administrators and reviewers to find efficiency and equity opportunities. We used a combination of primary and secondary research methods including contextual inquiries, surveys, pretotypes, semi-structured interviews, expert interviews as well as literature research in machine learning and competitive analysis. We synthesized the research findings using affinity diagraming, stakeholder & journey maps, and HMW questions.

Research Question 01
What are the different procedures, values, and stakeholders’ tasks in the admission process?
What role does ApplyGrad play in it?
RESEARCH METHOD
Contextual Inquiry With Administrators  

To begin our research into ApplyGrad, we set up contextual inquiries with administrators across various departments in SCS. We chose to interview administrators first because they hold the most knowledge of the department’s visions and rules. Our focus was to understand the different procedures, values, and stakeholders’ tasks in the admission process and the role that ApplyGrad plays in it. We obtained insights from eleven master’s programs representing six out of seven departments in SCS.

RESEARCH METHOD
Using Visual Models to Understand the System  

We created journey maps and stakeholder map for each interview we've conducted. Doing so allows us to see the connections between these stakeholders in the ApplyGrad ecosystem as well as find the commonalities amongst the various departments and programs. Based on the individual journey maps, we created a 'general' journal map to visualize the overall admission process in SCS across different departments.

Admin Journey Map

Key Insights

Use of External Tools

Many administrators and review committees use Excel and Google spreadsheets to filter and organize applicant data. They also use them to input their comments and evaluations.

Convergence & Divergence

While departments might have different criteria for evaluating applicants, their general review process is similar and want to make a right and fair decision

There Are Workarounds

Because they have been using ApplyGrad for several years, departments have various ways of training new users and additional tools that can help them accomplish functions that are not available in the system.

Research Question 02
How does the reviewers' experience in ApplyGrad look like?
What are their needs & pain points?
RESEARCH METHOD
Contextual Inquiry With Reviewers  

To understand reviewers' thought process when reviewing applications and how they use the ApplyGrad system, we conducted contextual inquiries with reviewers from four different programs. We developed fake application based on the middle applicants for each program. During the interview, we asked reviewer to review the fake application and think aloud in the process.

RESEARCH METHOD
Storyboard Speed Dating  

From the contextual inquiries, we developed a basic understanding of where the pain points lie and identified several design opportunities across different stages of the admissions process. To gather feedback on what direction to prioritize, we decided to use storyboards as our pretotype and speed-dated them with application reviewers.
Our storyboards are different from the traditional storyboards in that they each contained two empty frames for the participants to fill in during the session. Here we used the participatory design method and let users to help us identify hidden opportunities and potential design value through a co-creation process.

Each storyboard contained two empty frames for the participants to fill in

Key Insights

Effectiveness Over Efficiency

Reviewers valued effectiveness and fairness in application evaluation over making decisions quickly.

Overloading Reviewers

Reviewers felt frustrated over the amount of manual work required during the review process. There are opportunities for automation and ML intervention

No Prediction Please

Reviewers were not comfortable with an ML algorithm making predictions or judgements on applications. They were afraid that algorithm might introduce bias to the system.

Research Question 03
What are some opportunities to use advanced technologies such as Machine Learning (ML) to automate routine work?
Are there any concerns over the use of automation & ML?
RESEARCH METHOD
Expert Interview  

One of our client's long-term project visions is to help reviewers make more effective and efficient admission decisions with automation support. With the rapid advancement in ML, there also comes great responsibility to not exacerbate biases and unfairness. In order to comprehensively understand the opportunities and risks of integrating ML and automation into ApplyGrad, we interviewed experts in the following three areas: Technical details, Equity and fairness and ML as a design material. We also consulted experts in diversity, equity and inclusion (DEI) for recommendation on practices to ensure the fairness of the admission process.

RESEARCH METHOD
Competitive Analysis  

To understand how other platforms are approaching in similar areas, we performed a competitive analysis on similar systems to ApplyGrad. These platforms include Interfolio and Slate, which some SCS departments use for their admissions process, as well as the recruiting platform Element451.
One direction that we are exploring is an intervention to help reviewers keep track of their progress in committee meetings, or to remind them if there is an important component of an application that they have not yet addressed. Thus, we also looked at speech analysis platforms and speech-to-text AI systems such as Otter.ai, Primer.ai, Abridge, and MedRespond.

Key Insights

Human In-the-loop

It's important to combine ML and human intelligence to assist task automation and facilitate decision making.

Opportunities In DEI Support

Experts see huge opportunities in using ML to reduce bias and ensure fairness in the admission process.

Flexibility Is Important

Making the system customizable can cater to a variety of schools’ and programs’ needs and increase the flexibility of the system.
