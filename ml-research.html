Machine Learning Research

How can we use ML to help us?

ML Research Overview

Another goal of the project is to explore future advancements and integrations that will put ApplyGrad at the forefront of graduate application systems. Thus, we investigated opportunities to integrate advanced technologies such as Machine Learning and Automation to the review process.

We conducted expert interviews and literature review in three areas: Machine Learning, Equity & Fairness and ML as Design Materials. We also included questions in our contextual inquiry, speed-dating and contextual prototype testing sessions to learn about reviewers’ attitudes towards the use of ML in the admissions context.

Research Questions

We approach the domain of machine learning mostly to answer the following two questions:

  1. How can we better utilize machine learning to improve reviewers’ efficiency and effectiveness?
  2. What are the ideas and concerns reviewers have regarding integrating machine learning into ApplyGrad?

Insights


1.
The use of machine learning
should be focused on
improving efficiency in procedural tasks,
instead of decision making.


Reviewers see opportunities for ML and automation to help with time-consuming, tedious tasks. For example, in our speed dating, 3 out of 4 reviewers showed some interest in using ML to help extract information from applications. The most requested areas for help include extracting course information from transcripts, calculating work experience, keyword searching.

However, Reviewers don’t trust ML systems to make judgements because it may increase and exacerbate pre-existing bias. Reviewers value fairness in the review process and don’t want to sacrifice accuracy for efficiency. In our speed dating sessions, 3 out 4 reviewers expressed concerns with using ML to make recommendations. Reviewers preferred ML to play a facilitator role rather than directly make decisions.

“When machines affect humans, we have to be extremely careful."
–– Reviewer

In our expert interviews, ML Experts see more value in using ML to make the process more effective and fair than making the decisions. They saw more opportunities in using ML to ensure fairness of the review process. For example, tools could be designed to help reviewers calibrate reviews of letter of recommendations from different letter writers and check the existence of implicit bias.


2.
Efforts need to be put in
evaluating ML design using simulated data
before it’s applied into the real context.


Finding reliable datasets is critical to designing & prototyping ML models. It’s important to find reliable datasets for model development. The data labeling process needs to be closely monitored and models should be tested on adverse impacts.

Also, testing and simulation can help us better evaluate and communicate the cost and benefits of our design. In our speed dating sessions, 2 out of 4 reviewers mentioned that they only felt confident about using an ML-based solution if it has been tested in a simulation environment.

These findings point us to promising directions of integrating ML to ApplyGrad, which we will work on continuously in summer.
